Chapter 1: Salutation and the The Substance of Existence

1 Henry, a seed planted in Nigerian soil, to the Church scattered across the nations, the very planting of the Lord: Grace to you and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ, in whom we live and move and have our being.ᵃ

2 I write not with ink merely, but with the urgency of a fire shut up in my bones, calling to the deep that calls unto deep within you.

3 For the time is short, and the harvest is white; yet many of you sleep in the furrow, dreaming you are but dead earth, unaware that the Life of the Ages¹ pulses within your shell.

4 Hear this, my fellow fruits, and give ear, O sons of the Vine: You are not who you have been told you are.

5 Consider the man of old, the first [[ADAM|Adam]]. Was he merely dust? God forbid. For the Scripture says the Lord God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul.ᵇ

6 What is this mystery? It is the Strength of God dwelling in the blood of men. It is the Breath of the Ages clothed in dust and ashes.²

7 Therefore, know this: A human is not a beast with a higher mind. A human is a Templeᶜ with a divine Tenant. You are the meeting place where the Spirit touches the soil; you are the vessel where the Eternal Name rests.

8 But you say to me, "Brother, I am weak; I am full of faults; I am but a grasshopper in my own eyes."ᵈ

9 To this I answer: Do you judge the diamond by the dirt that covers it? Or do you judge the Ark by the badger skins that hide the gold?

10 You judge according to the flesh, but I know no man after the flesh.ᵉ I see the Treasure in the earthen vessel.ᶠ

11 For the Name of God is not a word you speak with your lips; it is the truth you carry in your veins. As He is, so are we in this world.ᵍ

12 Yet, I marvel that you have so quickly turned from him who called you to a gospel of emptiness. You walk as mere men,ʰ thinking you are orphans of the dust.

13 You have embraced vanity,³ chasing the wind, building cisterns that hold no water, forgetting that you are the Fountain.

14 Why do you beg for bread like beggars when the Bread of Life dwells in your house? Why do you cry out for a distant God when He is closer than your breath?

15 It is because a veil lies over your heart. You have forgotten the Rock from which you were hewn. You see the shadow and call it substance; you see the smoke and call it fire.

16 But behold, the mystery hidden from ages and generations is now made manifest to His saints:ⁱ God is not far from any one of us.

17 We are the One expressing as many.⁴ We are the branches of the single Vine.

18 You are not separate; you are particular. The wave is particular from the ocean, yet it is wet with the same water. So are you in Him.

19 Therefore, lift up your heads, O ye gates! Let the King of Glory come in, not from the sky above, but let Him arise from within the temple of your own being.

20 For if the root be holy, so are the branches. And if the Firstfruits is Christ, so is the lump.ʲ

21 You are the habitation of the Name. You are the burning bush that is not consumed. You are God in the Blood. Selah.

22 O the depth of the riches both of the wisdom and knowledge of God! How unsearchable are His judgments, and His ways past finding out!

23 For of Him, and through Him, and to Him, are all things: to whom be glory forever. Amen.

24 This is true. Selah.

25 But you ask, "If we are this glorious offspring, why do we suffer? Why do we die? Why was the seed buried in the dark earth of [[EDEN|Eden]]?"

The Infinite taking on Finitude. To say "I am just human" is a contradiction, for you cannot spell Human (Adam) without spelling God (Aleph). This is the structural reality of your being.

The separation you feel is a lie of the flesh; the unity is the truth of the Spirit.

Chapter 2: The Planting

1 What then of the Garden? What of Adam? What of the seed that Scripture says was planted in the ground?

2 Brethren, I would not have you ignorant concerning the mystery of the Fall. For you have been taught that Eden was a geography of dirt and trees, and that your father Adam was a failure who ruined a perfect plan.

3 But I say to you: God has no second thought. The Lamb was slain from the foundation of the world;ᵃ therefore, the Cross was not a remedy for failure, but the revelation of an eternal decree.

4 Consider the grain of wheat. Does the farmer weep when the seed falls from his hand into the dirt? Does he cry out, "Alas, the seed has fallen!"? No. He rejoices, for he knows the law of the harvest: unless the seed falls into the earth and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it bears much fruit.ᵇ

5 Eden was not merely a place; Eden was Sight.¹ It was the realm where the Eye was single, and the body was full of light. But the Seed had to be sown.

6 You will say to me, "Did not the serpent deceive the woman?" Indeed, the Scripture says she was deceived. But listen closely, you sons of the soil.

7 The Deceiver did not create the planting; the Master of the Harvest permitted the descent. For the creature was made subject to vanity, not willingly, but by reason of Him who subjected it in hope.ᶜ

8 The woman was led into the forgetting.² She moved from the union of knowing God to the divided mind of judging good and evil. She forgot she was the Daughter of the House and began to act as an orphan of the dust.

9 And when the cool of the day came, the Voice walked in the garden. And the Voice asked the question that echoes through every generation of men: "Where are you?"³

10 Do not think the All-Seeing Eye had lost His man. The Shepherd does not lose the sheep; the sheep loses the sight of the Shepherd.

11 God did not ask "Where are you?" to find a dwelling place. He asked to force the confession of condition. He was speaking to the Seed in the Soil: "In what state do you now exist? Do you realize you are buried?"

12 And Adam answered from the darkness of his forgetting: "I heard thy voice, and I was afraid, because I was naked; and I hid myself."ᵈ

13 Behold the weight of the Veil! God was still in the Blood, but the man could no longer see it. He looked at his capacity and saw only emptiness. He looked at his glory and saw only shame.

14 Therefore, the Lord God sent him forth. Why? Was it anger? Was it rejection? God forbid!

15 He sent him forth to till the ground from whence he was taken. He sent the Seed into the field. For if the man had eaten of the Tree of Life while trapped in the casing of the flesh, he would have become an immortal monster, a sinner forever, unable to die, unable to be reborn.

16 So He placed at the east of the garden Cherubim,⁴ and a flaming sword which turned every way, to keep the way of the Tree of Life.

17 O the severe mercy of God! You tremble at the Cherubim; you fear the sword. You say, "God has locked me out."

18 But I tell you, the Sword is not your enemy; the Sword is the Surgeon. It waits to cut the shell so the sprout can emerge. It waits to burn away the wood, hay, and stubble, so that only the Gold remains.ᵉ

19 And what of the curse? "Cursed is the ground for thy sake; in sorrow shalt thou eat of it."ᶠ

20 Hear this, my fellow seeds: The pressure of the soil is not the punishment of the judge; it is the work of the womb.

21 How shall the casing of the outer man break if the earth does not press against it? How shall the life escape the shell if the moisture and the weight do not crush it?

22 You feel the constriction; you feel the darkness. You cry out, "I am dying!" And the Husbandman answers, "Yes. You are dying. For that which you sow is not quickened, except it die."ᵍ

23 This is the mystery of [[MAVET|Mavet]].⁵ Death is not the end of your being; death is the bridge between the Sowing and the Reaping. Selah.

24 The Seed must lose its shape to find its nature. The Adam must lose his life to find it. The Shell must be crushed so the Tree can arise.

25 Therefore, do not despise the day of small things, nor the night of deep shadows. For the darkness and the light are both alike to Him.ʰ

26 But you ask, "Who shall deliver me from the body of this death? Who shall break the shell that is too hard for me?"

27 For we have tried to break ourselves, and we have only bruised our own flesh. We have tried to grow by straining, and we have only wearied our souls.

28 But God, who is rich in mercy, did not leave the Seed alone in the earth.

29 He spoke a promise in the darkness. He spoke of Another Seed. One who would come not to avoid the death, but to fill it.

30 One who would let the Serpent bruise His heel, that He might crush the Serpent's head. One who would enter the ultimate constriction, the ultimate silence, the ultimate soil.

31 We waited for the Branch. We waited for the Root of Jesse. We waited for the Name to put on blood and enter the mud with us.

32 And in the fullness of time, the Sower became the Seed.

33 This is the mystery hidden from the ages. This is the wisdom of the cross. Amen.

Chapter 3: The Name You Are

1 Brethren, having descended into the soil, and having tasted the bitterness of the heavy earth, do you now ask the question of the ages?

2 As Moses asked at the foot of the burning mountain, "Who am I, that I should go?" or "Who are You, that You should send me?"

3 Hear this, O you planted ones: You are not a label written on a page of history; you are a syllable in the mouth of the Living God.

4 For when the Prophet stood before the bush that burned but was not consumed, that bush which is the very pattern of your flesh¹, he asked for a Name.

5 And God did not give him a title to memorize; He gave him a Truth to inhabit.

6 "I WILL BE WHO I WILL BE."²

7 Consider the structure, my beloved. The Father is the Promise (I WILL BE); the Spirit is the Bond (WHO); the Son is the Fulfillment (I AM).

8 This is not a sign for your lips; it is the frame of your being.

9 For the Name is not outside of you, waiting to be called down; the Name is the Word that holds all things together.

10 If the Name were withdrawn, you would not merely die; you would vanish like smoke before the wind, for He upholds all things by the Word of His power.ᵃ

11 You will say to me, "But Henry, I am far from God; I am but dust and ashes, separated by my many sins."

12 O foolish branch! Who told you that you were separate from the Vine? Did the dust create itself?

13 Know ye not what the Scripture says? "In Him we live, and move, and have our being."ᵇ

14 There is but One Substance³ in all creation, and it is God.

15 If you exist, you exist in Him. There is no "outside" to the Eternal.

16 To think you are separate is the vanity of the mind; to know you are held is the beginning of wisdom.

17 Look at your own name: Adam. Are you not Blood filled with God?⁴

18 You are the Eternal dwelling in the mortal; you are the Treasure in the earthen vessel.

19 Therefore, do not blaspheme the Master by calling His dwelling place "empty."

20 For the Name of God is not sung alone; it is a harmony. It is Christ and the Church; it is the Head and the Body; it is God and Man in One New Man.⁵

21 Wake up, you who sleep in the dust! The veil is not on God's face; the veil is on your heart.ᶜ

22 The work of faith is not to move God toward you; it is to remove the shroud that blinds you to His presence.

23 For we do not become the Sons of God by striving; we are awakened to Sonship by remembering.⁶

24 The world sees you as common wood, dry and brittle.

25 But the Spirit sees the Gold overlay; He sees the Bush that burns with the Fire of the Age to Come.

26 As He is, so are we in this world.ᵈ

27 Not "as He was," nor "as He will be," but as He is.

28 He is the Beloved Son; therefore, you are the Beloved. He is the Heir; therefore, you are Joint-Heirs.

29 This is the testimony of the Blood: that the Name includes you.

30 Stand, therefore, not as beggars outside the gate, but as priests within the veil.

31 O the depth of the riches both of the wisdom and knowledge of God!

32 From Him is the Source, through Him is the Process, and to Him is the Revealing.

33 To the One who is All in All, be glory in the Church by Christ Jesus throughout all ages, world without end. Amen.

Chapter 4: The Pattern for a Temple of Flesh

1 Brethren, builders of the House: You hold the scroll in your hands and see a whip; but I tell you, it is a mirror.

2 For the Law is not a list of restrictions for a servant; it is the description of the capacity of a Son.

3 You say, "God demands perfection that I might be saved." But I say, "God requires integrity that you might be filled."

4 Consider the archer. Does he hate the arrow that falls short? No, he corrects the wind. For the instruction¹ is not a burden to the shoulder, but a path for the arrow.

5 Know ye not that you are the Tabernacle of witness? The curtains are your flesh, the pillars are your bones, and the Holy of Holies is the chamber of your heart where the Spirit dwells.ᵃ

6 Therefore, when the Builder speaks, He is not limiting your freedom; He is specifying your dimensions.

7 He speaks "Do not," and you hear "I am angry." But the Spirit says, "Build the wall here, so the roof does not collapse."

8 For a city without walls is broken down,ᵇ and a vessel without boundaries cannot hold the Wine.

9 What then of perfection? You say, "I am flawed; I am broken."

10 But listen to the ancient command given to our father Abraham: "Walk before me, and be blameless."²

11 He did not say "be sinless in time"; He said "be whole in frame."

12 For a cup that is clean on the outside but cracked in the clay will spill the vintage upon the ground.

13 To be double-minded is to be a cracked vessel. To serve two masters is to have a breach in the hull.

14 The hypocrite is not merely a liar; he is structurally unsound. He cannot bear the weight.

15 Therefore, purify your hearts, you double-minded,ᶜ not to appease an angry Judge, but to seal the vessel³ for the outpouring.

16 Now concerning the Fire.

17 You tremble at the word "Holy." You think it means "morally superior."

18 But I tell you, Holiness⁴ is the measure required of the human temple.

19 Our God is a consuming fire.ᵈ Not a fire of malice, but a fire of substance.

20 The bush that burns and is not consumed is the pattern. The wood is common; the fire is holy. Why does the wood not burn? Because it is compatible with the flame.

21 You say to me, "We are not under the Law!"

22 I answer you: The shadow is gone, but the engineering remains. We do not keep the feast of shadows; we are the feast. We do not build the temple of stone; we are the temple.

23 Nadab and Abihu offered strange fire and were consumed, not because God hated them, but because they tried to carry the Holy One with uncovered hands.

24 Therefore, be ye holy, for He is holy.ᵉ Seal the vessel. Prepare the room.

25 Do not let the profane (the common, the empty) touch the weighty. Do not yoke the ox with the donkey. Do not mingle the wool with the linen.

26 This is not ritual; this is harmony.

27 For the end of the commandment is not a behavior; it is a body so filled with Light that the face shines like the sun on the mountain.ᶠ

28 You are the instrument. He is the Player. Be tuned. Amen.

29 And having been built, how then shall we walk through the valley of the shadow?

Chapter 5: The Walk of the Crushed

1 My fellow seeds, wrestling in the dark soil: I know your groaning. You say, "The earth is too heavy; the darkness is too thick; surely God has forgotten us in this grave."

2 But I ask you: Does the olive yield its oil without the press? Does the grape yield its wine without the crushing?

3 You call this the "Evil Day," but the Spirit calls it the Day of the Strait.¹ You feel the walls closing in, the breath leaving your lungs, the terror of the narrow place.

4 Hear this mystery: The narrowness is not the end of your life; it is the beginning of your expansion. For God uses the narrow place to birth the wide place.²

5 You cry out against the adversary, saying, "The enemy surrounds me!" But I tell you, even the winnowing fork is in the Master's hand. The enemy intends to consume you, but he serves only to crack the shell you could not break yourself.

6 He thinks he is your destroyer; he knows not that he is your midwife. For without the pressure of the soil, the Seed remains alone; but with the pressure, the Life is forced upward.

7 Therefore, do not despise the squeezing. For when the enemy presses you into the dust, he is merely planting you deeper into the Substance.

8 What then? Shall we lie down and die? God forbid! We enter the Rest that works.

9 For there is a Rest³ that is not sleep. There is a stillness that moves faster than light. It is the silence of the center while the wheel turns; it is the eye of the hurricane while the winds rage.

10 Moses gave you a day of rest; Christ gives you a life of rest. For the Sabbath was but a shadow; the Substance is the absence of friction.

11 Why do you toil, O Sons of the Dust? Why do you spin your wheels against the Eternal? The "Man of the Flesh" trembles with anxiety, thinking he must sustain the world.

12 But the "Man of the Spirit" has anchored his vessel into the River. He does not row; he is carried. He does not strive; he abides.

13 Cease your striving to be what you already are. Cease reaching for where you already stand. The bird does not study the ways of flight to fly; it yields to the wind. Yield to the Spirit.

14 You say, "But I must pray! I must besiege heaven!"

15 Brethren, do you think prayer is the convincing of a reluctant God? Do you think your many words will wake Him who never sleeps?

16 True prayer is not begging; true prayer is Remembrance.⁴

17 For we have forgotten our Name. The amnesia of the Fall lies thick upon our minds like a cataract. We look in the mirror and see a slave, an orphan, a grasshopper.

18 Therefore, we take up the Word, not as a law book, but as a sharp sword. We pray to cut through the lie. We pray to remember what was written in the blood before the foundation of the world.

19 This is the work of the Spirit: to bring to your remembrance all things. To wake you from the nightmare of separation.

20 Do not speak to God as a stranger. Speak to Him as your Source.

21 When the world screams "Lack," and your eyes see "Empty," do not agree with the lie. Speak the Same Word⁵ that God speaks.

22 He says, "You are Righteous." Do you say, "I am trying to be"? Then you are a liar.

23 He says, "You are Healed." Do you say, "I am sick"? Then you have broken the alignment.

24 To confess is to say the same thing. It is to tune your vessel with the Word of Heaven until the sound breaks the vessel of your circumstances.

25 Be constant in this. For the Covenant is perpetual.⁶ Your feelings are the waves; His faithfulness is the Ocean. Do not trust the waves.

26 Look not at the things which are seen, for they are vapor; but at the things which are not seen, for they are iron.

27 You are the Temple. You are the Seed. You are the Beloved.

28 Stand, therefore, in the wide place, even while the walls press in. For the pressure is temporary, but the Glory is eternal.

29 But some of you are still blinded. Some of you still hold to the shadows, fearing the light.

30 What is this veil that remains? What is this curtain that men have stitched back together? Turn the page, and let us tear it down.

Just as the birth canal narrows to force the baby into the world, Tzar is the pressure God permits to force the soul out of the "Womb of Comfort" into the "World of Purpose." Jesus entered the ultimate Tzar (the Cross) to bring us into ultimate freedom. See Psalm 118:5; Psalm 4:1.
    
2. Wide Place ,  Hebrew: [[YASHA|YASHA]] (יָשַׁע). The root of Yeshua (Jesus). It literally means "To make wide," "To create space," or "To deliver from constraint." Salvation is not just a ticket to heaven; it is the structural widening of your reality. When you are in Tzar (tightness), you cry out, and God answers with Yasha (spaciousness). He pushes back the walls of your limitation. See Psalm 18:19; Psalm 118:5.
    
3. Rest ,  Hebrew: [[MENUHA|MENUHA]] (מְנוּחָה). Often associated with sleep, but defined as the "Absence of Friction" or "Tranquility." It is the state where the internal conflict of the outer man ceases. The "Man in the Soil" trembles with anxiety (toil); the "New Man" operates in Menuha. It is the state of the Seed accepting the Soil without fighting it. It is docking your finite vessel into the Infinite Flow so that His power becomes your motion. See Isaiah 66:1; Hebrews 4:3.
    
4. Surgery/Remembrance ,  Hebrew: ZAKAR (זָכָר). This is not passive recollection; it is "Surgical Remembrance." The word shares a root with "Male" (the one who marks/cuts). It implies using the Sword of Truth to cut through the amnesia of the Fall (Nasha). Prayer is the act of Zakar, piercing the lie of separation to access the reality of Union. We do not pray to change God; we pray to cut away the forgetfulness that blinds us to Him. See Psalm 77:11; Luke 22:19.
    
5. Same Word ,  Greek: [[HOMOLOGEO|HOMOLOGEO]] (ὁμολογέω). From Homo (same) + [[LOGOS|Logos]] (word). To "confess" is to "speak the same thing." It is not informing God of your sins or needs; it is aligning your speech with His Reality. When God says, "You are forgiven," and you say, "I am forgiven," you are in Homologeo. You are harmonizing your sound with the Truth. This alignment is the means of revealing. See 1 John 1:9; Hebrews 4:14.
    
6. Perpetual ,  Hebrew: [[TEMID|TEMID]] (תָּמִיד). The "Constant." Used for the fire on the altar and the bread of the Presence. It represents the Steady State of God's faithfulness (Yesh). Our emotions fluctuate; our performance varies; but the Temid of the Covenant remains unbroken. We walk securely not because we are constant, but because He is. Faith is simply the decision to trust the Temid over the temporary. See Exodus 27:20; Isaiah 58:11.

Chapter 6: The Prophetic Breaking Forth

1 I would not have you ignorant, brethren, concerning the great concealment. For there is a covering cast over all people, and a veil that is spread over all nations.ᵃ But know this: the covering is not a wall of stone; it is a woven fabric of the mind.

2 Why do you love the curtain? You stand in the outer court, trembling at the smoke, paying the priest to enter where you fear to tread. You say to me, "The Glory is too heavy; let Moses speak to us, lest we die."

3 But I say to you: The day of Moses is past. The Glory is not a fire that consumes the son; it is the warmth that incubates the heir.

4 Consider the Temple. For ages, the Veil¹ stood between the Holy Place and the Holiest of All. It was a mercy, for the flesh was not yet ready to bear the weight. But when the Last Adam cried out, "It is finished," what happened to the curtain?

5 It was torn. Not from bottom to top, as by the hand of man, but from top to bottom, by the finger of God.ᵇ

6 Therefore, he who stitches the veil back together denies the work of the Cross. He who preaches separation preaches a gospel of the old priesthood. We do not have a High Priest who cannot be touched; we have a Forerunner who has anchored us behind the veil.ᶜ

7 This is the mystery of the Revealed²: The Torn Veil is the Flesh of Christ. And if you are in Him, you are already on the other side of the separation.

8 But we all, with unveiled face, beholding as in a mirror the glory of the Lord, are being transformed into the same image.ᵈ Not by striving, but by beholding. This is true. Amen.

9 Why then do the shepherds cry "Distance! Distance!"? Why do they build walls where God has torn the fabric?

10 Beware the merchants³ of the gap. They profit from your amnesia. They sell you water while you stand waist-deep in the river. They sell you maps to a city where you already dwell.

11 For it is easier to pick the fruit of religion than to dig for the Root of the Name. The fruit is sweet for a moment, but it has no life in itself. The Root requires the sweat of the soul; it requires the shovel of truth to break the hard ground of tradition.

12 And what is the Root? It is the scandal of the ages. It is the secret that makes the religious tremble. It is the confession: "God is in the blood." The Life of the Creator is the substance of the creature.

13 O the offense of it! To tell a man he is a worm is easy; he will nod and pay his tithe. But to tell a man he is the Temple? To tell him he is the habitation of the Name? He will either worship in spirit and truth, or he will pick up a stone to kill you.ᵉ

14 Did they not stone the prophets for this? Did they not crucify the Lord of Glory because He made Himself one with the Father?

15 If they persecuted the Head for claiming Sonship, shall they not persecute the Body for claiming inheritance? Yet, fear not. The servant is not greater than his master.

16 I charge you therefore, before God and the Lord Jesus Christ: Do not trade your birthright for a bowl of religious soup.ᶠ Do not trade the Truth of the Son for the shadow of the slave.

17 For the hypocrite loves the mask. He prays on the corner to be seen by men, for his reward is the applause of the flesh. But you, children of the Day, you have no need of masks.

18 The Revealing⁴ demands nakedness. You cannot reveal the Son while wearing the costume of the orphan.

19 Therefore, take heed: The tearing of the veil was not a polite invitation; it was a divine invasion. The Holy of Holies is no longer safe from the presence of man, nor is man safe from the presence of God.

20 To live unveiled is to live without defense. It is to let the Fire burn the chaff of the false self until only the Gold remains. You say, "I am afraid to be seen." But I say to you, "You are already known." The Hidden is finished; the Revealed is now.

21 Do not stitch up what God has torn. Do not build a fence where He has opened a Door. For if you rebuild the wall of separation, you make Christ a transgressor.ᵍ

22 For the creation stands on tiptoe, waiting. It does not wait for better sermons. It does not wait for louder songs. It waits for the unveiling of the Sons of God.

23 This is the Prophetic Disruption. It is not the noise of the crowd; it is the silence of the Son standing up in the soul of the believer. It is the Seed breaking the soil, no longer apologizing for its nature.

24 But how does this glory walk on two legs? How does the Mystery wear the clothes of the mother, the pastor, and the child? How does the Helper speak, and how does the Shepherd lead? Let us now look at the order of the House.

Christ's death tore the Veil, signaling that the germination is complete and the Sprout has emerged. See Exodus 26:31-33; Hebrews 10:19-20.

The entire biblical narrative moves from Nistar (hidden reality in God's mind) to Nigleh (visible revealing in history). See Romans 8:19; Colossians 3:4.

To charge for access to the Father is to deny the finished work of the Son. Jesus drove them from the Temple, for they had made the Father's house a "house of merchandise" (John 2:16). The modern equivalent is any system that profits from keeping believers in a state of separation rather than sonship. See Matthew 21:12; 2 Corinthians 2:17.
    
4. Revealing ,  Greek: [[PHANEROO|PHANEROO]] (φανερόω). This is the key to Incarnational theology. It means to make visible what was already present but hidden. The Cross did not create salvation; it manifested the eternal decision. You do not pray to get God; you pray to manifest the God who is already there. The believer's life is the ongoing Phaneroo, making visible in time what has always been true in eternity. See 1 John 1:2; Colossians 1:26; 1 John 3:2.
    
5. Apokalypsis ,  Greek: APOKALYPSIS (ἀποκάλυψις). Literally "to take off the cover." Often translated "revelation" or "apocalypse." The creation is not waiting for God to come down; it is waiting for the cover to be taken off you. The unveiling of the Sons of God is the moment when the Church finally realizes and manifests who she has been all along. This is not arrogance; this is awakening. See Romans 8:19; Revelation 1:1.

Chapter 7: Specific Exhortations

1 Therefore, if the Veil is torn, and if the Name rests upon the whole House, how shall we walk? Shall the foot say to the hand, "I have no need of you"? Or shall the man say to the woman, "You are an afterthought"? God forbid.

2 For in the beginning, the Deep Sleep fell upon the First Adam, and from his side came the Life. In the fullness of time, the Deep Sleep of death fell upon the Last Adam, and from His pierced side came the Water and the Blood.ᵃ As Eve was to Adam, so the Church is to Christ: not a separate creation, but the same substance brought forth to be the Mirror.

3 To the Women, the Mothers of the Living:

4 Do not let the world tell you that you are secondary. You are the Warrior who stands face-to-face¹. You were not taken from the foot to be trampled, nor from the head to rule, but from the side, the chamber of the heart, the structural beam that holds the House together.

5 You say, "I am weak." But I say to you: The Helper is the rescue sent when human strength fails. It is the very title of God when He rides to deliver Israel.ᵇ You are the Watchman at the gate of life; you are the Receiver who transforms the Seed into the Fruit. Without the Womb, the Seed remains alone.

6 Therefore, stand in your dignity. You are the one who encompasses². As the Church surrounds Christ with praise, so you surround the family with prayer. You are the living sign of the Bride who makes herself ready.

7 To the Men, the Bearers of the Name:

8 Remember the law of the harvest: The Seed must die to live. You are called to be the one who Remembers³, the one who Cuts the Covenant, the one who Lays Down his life.

9 Do not lord it over the heritage of God. For the Head is not the one who crushes; the Head is the source of provision. Did Christ crush the Church? No, He washed her with the water of the Word.ᶜ So must you wash your house, not with the vinegar of accusation, but with the water of Identity. Speak the Name over them until they glow.

10 For in the Lord, neither is the man independent of the woman, nor the woman independent of the man.ᵈ We are the Echad expressing through two faces. One Substance, particular callings.

11 To the Pastors and Shepherds:

12 Hear me, you who watch for souls. Why do you beat the sheep? Why do you construct fences of fear to keep them in the pen? If you tell them they are sinners, they will sin to prove you right. But if you tell them they are Kadosh. Holy Vessels, they will sanctify themselves to hold the Glory.

13 The Torah is not a whip; it is a Mirror. Stop preaching outward change; start preaching True Transformation. When the Prince knows he is a Prince, he no longer eats from the trough of the pigs.

14 Do not be merchants of the Veil. Do not sell access to a God who is already inside the room. Your job is not to bridge the gap; your job is to announce that the Bridge has already been laid. Feed them the meat of the Word until they realize they are the Temple.

15 For the time is short, and the deception is thick. The flock needs the Lion of Judah, not the morality of the Pharisees. Show them the Christ in them, the Hope of Glory.ᵉ

16 To the Youth and the Students:

17 You stand between two worlds. The West tells you the universe is a machine, dead and empty. Your ancestors told you the universe was alive, full of spirits in the wind and the water.

18 Listen to the wisdom of the Name: The world is not God, but God is the Substance of the world. Do not trade your spiritual heritage for the dry bread of earthly treasures.

19 The white man's science measures the shell, but it cannot find the Life. Do not let them convince you that you are an accident of matter. You are God in the Blood. You are a living vessel designed to house the Most High.

20 Do not fear the old spirits, and do not serve them. For you are the habitation of the Most High. The Spirit that moved upon the face of the waters now moves in your veins. You are the Completion. You are the Fulfillment.

21 Therefore, do not be ashamed of the Mystery. When they mock the invisible, remember: the visible is made of the invisible.ᶠ They study the shadows on the wall; you know the One who casts the Light.

22 Stand firm. Hold the line. You are the generation of the Revealing. The Fathers planted in tears; you shall reap in power.

23 What then is the conclusion of the matter? The Male, the Female, the Elder, the Youth, we are members of one another. We are the Body of the Mashiach. And the eye cannot say to the hand, "I have no need of you."

24 We move together. We rise together. We are the Echad. Amen.

Chapter 8: The Perfection of Love

1 Finally, my beloved, hear the conclusion of the whole matter. Though I speak with the tongues of men and of angels, and understand all mysteries and all knowledge, if I have not Love, I am become sounding brass.

2 For the Name which we bear is not a sword to divide, but a cord to bind. We have spoken of the Seed, and the Soil, and the Temple; yet all these are but shadows of the one true substance.

3 And what is that substance? It is the very nature of the Father poured out.

4 Consider the mystery: The wisdom of this world seeks to divide, saying, "This is this, and that is that." It draws lines in the sand and builds walls of separation. It says God is Light and cannot dwell in darkness.

5 Yet behold the Wisdom of God! Did He not dwell in the thick darkness?ᵃ Did not the High and Lofty One make His bed in Sheol?ᵇ Is He not the Lion who is the Lamb? Is He not the Lord of All who became the Servant of all?

6 This is the stumbling block to the fleshly mind. The natural man seeks a God he can measure, a God who fits within the narrow room of his own understanding. He says, "God is either here or there."

7 But we speak the wisdom of God in a mystery, even the hidden wisdom,¹ which God ordained before the world unto our glory. For in Christ, the fullness of the Godhead dwells bodily. He is the Cornerstone where two walls meet; He is the Peace who hath made both one.

8 In Him, the First is the Last. In Him, the Master is the Servant. In Him, the death is the life. He is the reconciliation of all things. Amen.

9 Therefore, little children, flee from the tree that brings death. You know of which tree I speak. It is the tree where the head sees for itself, dividing Good from Evil, Friend from Enemy, Holy from Profane.

10 This is the knowledge that puffeth up. It is the accounting of the scribe who measures the distance between the dust and the star.

11 But I call you to a higher tree. I call you to Union.²

12 For to "know" the Lord is not to hold thoughts of Him in your mind, as one holds water in a sieve. Did not the scripture say, "Adam knew Eve his wife"? This knowing is union. This knowing is the mingling of breath. This knowing is the collapse of the two into the One.

13 When you know Him as He is, you shall be like Him. You shall not see Him as a stranger afar off, but as the Life of your life, the Breath of your breath, the Substance of your Substance.

14 O the depth of the riches both of the wisdom and knowledge of God! How unsearchable are His judgments, and His ways past finding out!

15 And now, gaze into the mirror of the Word. What do you see? Do you see a solitary man, striving to reach heaven? God forbid!

16 You see the Name. And the Name is not a word you speak; it is a truth you inhabit.

17 For if God is One, and if He fills all in all, then where does your brother stand? Is he outside of God? Is he outside of the Name?

18 Hear this, you who are heirs of the Promise: You cannot love the Father whom you have not seen, if you hate the brother whom you have seen.ᶜ For your brother is the vessel of the Name.

19 The truth of the Kingdom is this: The Name of God is You and I. Not you alone. Not I alone. But us, together, in compound unity.³

20 When you look upon the poor, the broken, and even the enemy at the gate, you are looking upon the soil where the Seed is hidden. Do not curse the ground that holds the treasure.

21 We are the One expressing as the Many. We are the Body of the Invisible God made visible. This is the mystery which has been hid from ages and from generations, but now is made manifest to His saints.

22 Therefore, let your love be without dissimulation. Let your rest be without striving. Let your work be without anxiety. For it is God which worketh in you both to will and to do of His good pleasure.

23 The veil is torn. The way is open. The separation was a dream; the Union is the Truth.

24 Awake, you who sleep! Arise from the dead thoughts of separation, and Christ shall give you light.

25 We are the generation of the Revealing.⁴ We are the Sons of the Revealing. The earth groans for our appearing. Let us not disappoint the creation.

26 Now unto Him who is the First and the Last, the Beginning and the End, the Root and the Offspring.

27 To the One who planted the Seed in the darkness of the ages, that He might reap the Harvest in the light of the Day.

28 To the Father who is the Source of all Being. To the Son who is the Revealing of all Glory. To the Spirit who is the Breath of all Life.

29 To the King eternal, immortal, invisible, the only wise God, be honor and glory for ever and ever.

30 The Grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. You are His House. You are His Body. You are His Name.

31 Amen.
