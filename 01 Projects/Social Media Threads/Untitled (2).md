   # **Untitled**

## Chapter 1: The Seed in the Soil

**1** Henry, a seed sown in the dark soil of Nigeria, yet held secure in the eternal Granary of the Father, and a servant of the Mystery which was hidden for ages but is now made manifest. **2** To the Church in Lagos, and to the scattered shoots of God emerging through the red earth of every nation: to those who are sanctified in the thought of God before they breathed the air of earth. **3** Grace to you, which is the substance of the End present in the Beginning, and peace, which is the cessation of the friction of the self.

  

**4** I write this not as a master over your faith, but as a fellow branch grafted into the same Vine, bearing the same sap, groaning with the same desire for the breaking of the shell. **5** For we do not write to you with ink merely, but with the pressure of the Spirit¹ who engraves the Truth upon the tablets of the heart. **6** Acknowledge the Name² that is upon you; not the names given by men, nor the titles conferred by institutions, but the Name which is the very texture of your existence.

  

**7** I thank my God always concerning you, not looking at the dust that clings to your feet, but beholding the Gold that lines the walls of your inner sanctuary. **8** For I do not cease to give thanks for the Reality that sustains you, even when your eyes are yet dim to perceive it. **9** Men look at the husk and say, "It is dry and shapeless," but God looks at the seed and says, "Here is the forest." **10** Therefore, I thank God that you are rich in Him, having been filled with all substance³ before you ever performed a single work to purchase it.

  

**11** For who made you differ from another? And what have you that you did not receive? **12** If then you received it, why do you labor as though you must create it? **13** It is a strange thing I see under the sun: kings walking as servants, and sons begging for bread in their own Father's house. **14** Yet I thank God that the Amnesia⁴ is breaking. **15** I thank Him that the noise of the market and the chaos of the city cannot drown out the whisper of the Blood that speaks better things than the blood of Abel.

  

**16** For the blood of Abel cried from the ground for vengeance, but the Blood of the Last [[ADAM|Adam]] cries from the ground of your own being for recognition.

  

**17** Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has blessed us with every spiritual blessing in the heavenly places in Christ. **18** Consider this, brethren! He chose us in Him before the foundation of the worldᵃ. **19** Before there was a Lagos, before there was a Niger, before there was a dust to form a man, there was a Decree. **20** You were not an afterthought of creation; you were the blueprint of the ages held in the Mind of the Architect.

  

**21** For knowing this, that our old man was crucified with Himᵇ, we understand that the history of your life is not merely the story of your days on earth. **22** Your history began in the Silence. **23** O the depth of the riches both of the wisdom and knowledge of God! How unsearchable are His judgments and His ways past finding out! **24** For He hid the Fruit inside the Seed, and He hid the Glory inside the Clay, that no flesh should glory in His presence, but that the Treasure⁵ might be all in all.

  

**25** Therefore, I do not write to teach you a new thing, as the Greeks seek to hear some new philosophy. **26** I write to stir up your pure minds by way of remembrance. **27** I write to cut through the thick callous of forgetfulness that has settled upon your hearts. **28** For you have not lost your identity; you have only lost your sight of it. **29** The sun does not cease to be a ball of fire because a cloud passes before it; neither does your sonship cease to be a reality because the shadow of the flesh falls upon it.

  

**30** But you say, "Henry, we see only corruption. We see only the struggle. We see the dust." **31** Tell me, you who read the Law, do you not hear the Law? **32** Does a seed planted in the ground complain of the darkness? **33** Does it not know that the weight of the soil is the very force required to break the shell? **34** You act as though the darkness is a mistake. **35** You pray for the removal of the soil, not knowing that the soil is the servant of the harvest.

  

**36** For if the planting was an accident, then the harvest is a gamble. **37** But if the planting was the wisdom of God, then the breaking of the shell is the glory of the Son. **38** What then of the Garden? What of Adam? What of the seed that Scripture says was planted in the ground? Hear now what was hidden from the beginning.

You are already perfect in the Nistar state (Colossians 3:3); your life on earth is the developing process ([[PHANEROO|Phaneroo]]) of making that hidden reality visible.

  

## Chapter 2: The Intentional Planting

**1** Brethren, I would not have you ignorant concerning the mystery of the beginning. For many teachers have told you that the story of man is a tragedy of an accidental fall, as if the Almighty were surprised by the creature of His own making.

  

**2** But I ask you: Does the Architect of the Ages build without a blueprint? Does the Omniscient One stumble into history reacting to the errors of dust?

  

**3** God forbid! For it is written that the Lamb was slain from the foundation of the world.ᵃ If the remedy was prepared before the foundation, then the malady was known, measured, and encompassed within the decree of the King.

  

**4** Therefore, we must not look at [[EDEN|Eden]] as a paradise lost by mistake, but as a nursery where the Seed¹ was prepared for planting.

  

**5** Consider the wisdom of the Husbandman. Does a farmer keep his precious grain in the barn forever? Is the seed safe on the shelf? Yes, it is safe, but it is alone. It abides in its own perfection, yet it bears no fruit.

  

**6** But to bring forth a harvest, the farmer must do a violent thing. He must cast the seed into the cold, dark, heavy soil. He must subject it to the burial.

  

**7** Hear the words of the Lord Jesus, which unlock the mystery of Adam: "Unless a grain of wheat falls into the ground and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it produces much grain."ᵇ

  

**8** O the depth of this mystery! We have thought this spoke only of Calvary, but behold, it speaks also of Eden. The First Adam was the grain that fell; the Last Adam is the Life that rose.

  

**9** Therefore, the expulsion from the Garden was not merely a punishment; it was a planting. The Man was not banished from God’s presence solely for wrath’s sake; he was sown into the soil of mortality² to undergo the necessary death of the outer shell.

  

**10** For you are not merely biological accidents struggling in Lagos traffic; you are the planting of the Lord, carrying the Divine Life encased in heavy flesh.

  

**11** Why then do you mourn the soil? Why do you despise the pressure of this present age? Do you not know that the weight of the earth is what cracks the shell to release the sprout?

  

**12** The Cherubim³ were not placed to keep you out of life forever, but to ensure you did not re-enter as a Shell. They guard the way until the breaking is complete, until the Seed becomes the Fruit.

  

**13** Consider the "Fall." Was it a failure of God’s plan? No. It was the transition from the shelf to the soil. The Seed had to be subjected to vanity, not willingly, but by reason of Him who subjected it in hope.ᶜ

  

**14** This is why the veil⁴ lies heavy upon the nations. You have been taught that this forgetfulness is a curse, but I tell you, it is the darkness of the soil.

  

**15** A seed in the ground cannot see the sun. It knows only pressure, darkness, and moisture. It feels abandoned. It feels as though it is dying.

  

**16** And indeed, it _is_ dying. But what is dying? The husk is dying. The shell is rotting. The false definition of "self" that stands apart from God is being crushed.

  

**17** But the Life⁵ inside—the [[ZERA|Zera]]—cannot die, for it is the very substance of God. It is imperishable.

  

**18** Therefore, death is not the end of your story; death is the connector. It is the chaotic bridge between the sowing and the reaping.

  

**19** Look at your own lives. You strive to be "good," you strive to be "holy," yet you feel the weakness of the flesh. You cry out, "Wretched man that I am!"

  

**20** But I say to you: Peace. Be still. The shell _must_ fail. The outer man _must_ perish so that the inner man may be renewed day by day.ᵈ

  

**21** Do not despise the darkness of your planting. Do not curse the heavy Nigerian soil that presses against you. For in this very pressure, the Husbandman is at work.

  

**22** The Enemy, the dark one, thinks he is burying you to destroy you. He does not know he is merely the spade in the hand of the Gardener. He digs the hole, but God plants the Seed.

  

**23** What then? Shall we sin that grace may abound? God forbid! We do not celebrate the rot of the shell, but neither do we fear it.

  

**24** We recognize that we are in the season of the sprouting. The husk of the First Adam is breaking open, and the vibrant life of the Last Adam is pushing through.

  

**25** You were chosen in Him before the foundation of the world. You were held in the Nistar—the hidden silence of eternity—before you were ever manifest in the noise of time.

  

**26** So when you feel the amnesia, when you feel you have forgotten God, do not despair. This forgetting is the necessary sleep—the Tardemah⁶—from which you shall awaken as the Bride.

  

**27** For God has shut up all in disobedience, that He might have mercy on all.ᵉ He allowed the displacement of your mind so that the restoration would be by grace and not by your own holding.

  

**28** O the wisdom of God! Who would have thought to bury His own Image in dust to multiply it? Who would have thought to clothe His glory in the skins of animals?

  

**29** Therefore, brethren, lift up your heads. You are not a mistake. You are not a failed experiment. You are the Seed of God, sown in the field of the world, destined for a harvest that will shake the heavens.

  

**30** Do not let the religious rulers bind you with fear of the Fall. The Fall was the gravity that pulled the Seed into the furrow. Now, the Resurrection is the power that pulls the Fruit toward the Sun.

  

**31** We are that Fruit. We are the sprouting taking place in the midst of the decay.

  

**32** But how does this sprout emerge? How does the man in the soil know he is more than dirt? He must hear the Voice. He must remember the Name.

## Chapter 3: The Name You Are, Not The Name You Call

**1** Brethren, I would not have you ignorant concerning the great mystery of the Name. **2** You ask, "What is the Name of the Lord?" as if it were a password to open a gate or a label to summon a deity from afar. **3** But I tell you a mystery: The Name is not a vocabulary word for your tongue; it is a housing for your spirit. **4** Moses stood at the burning bush and asked, "Who shall I say sent me?" and the Almighty did not give him a label to analyze but a reality to inhabit: "I WILL BE what I WILL BE."ᵃ **5** Therefore, understand this: God does not have a Name that is separate from His existence; He IS the Name, and you are called _by_ that Name because you are held _within_ that Name.

  

**6** Why then do you stand outside the gate, begging like orphans for bread, when you are the very offspring of the House? **7** You treat the Almighty like a merchant, thinking your prayers are coins and His blessings are wares to be bought with tears and fasting. **8** O foolish Galatians! O weary saints of Lagos! Who has bewitched you to trade your sonship for servitude? **9** You cry out to Him across a distance that does not exist. **10** For in Him we live, and move, and have our being;ᵇ we are not merely near Him—we are the fish swimming in the Ocean of His substance.

  

**11** Hear now the foundation of our confession: The Name of God is not what you call Him; it is who you are to Him.¹ **12** Did not the Lord Jesus say, "I have manifested Your Name to the men You gave Me"?ᶜ **13** Yet, did He teach them a new pronunciation? Did He teach them a new syllable? **14** No! He revealed the Father. He revealed the love wherewith He was loved. He revealed the Name by _being_ the Son. **15** Therefore, to know the Name is not to speak Hebrew correctly; it is to stand in the posture of the Son and receive the love of the Father.

  

**16** Consider the structure of the Divine Signature.² It is the Hand (Yod) engaging in Work, the Revelation (Hey) of that Work, connected by the Nail (Vav) to the final Manifestation (Hey). **17** Do you not see? The Name describes the process of the Infinite becoming the Finite, of the Seed entering the Soil. **18** You are involved in the Name. You are the location where the Name is being hallowed. **19** When you say "Hallowed be Thy Name," you are not wishing for God to be famous; you are agreeing that your own vessel is set apart to house His weight.

  

**20** What then of faith? Is it a mental strain? Is it the human will trying to convince the Divine Mind? **21** God forbid! Faith is not you generating belief in a distant object. **22** Faith is the Spirit of the Son within you recognizing the Father. **23** It is Deep calling unto Deep.ᵈ It is the God _in_ you agreeing with the God _above_ you. **24** When you believe, it is not a human work; it is the "Amen" of the internal Seed echoing the "Yea" of the eternal Promise. **25** This is the confession³—not merely admitting facts, but speaking the same word (_Homologeo_) that God has already spoken over you.

  

**26** And what of prayer? You weary yourselves with many words, thinking you will be heard for your striving. **27** But true prayer is not petitioning an external deity to intervene; true prayer is the Womb⁴ of God speaking through His own boundaries. **28** It is the Breath (Ruach) returning to the Breather. **29** When you pray in the Spirit, it is the Divine Circuit completing itself through your lungs. **30** Stop trying to bridge a gap that Christ has already filled with His own body! The veil is torn! There is no "outside" to God!

  

**31** Therefore, you are not trying to become righteous; you _are_ the righteousness of God in Christ.ᵉ **32** A branch does not try to become part of the vine; it rests in the flow of the sap. **33** If you are in the Name, you are in the Righteousness. **34** O the depth of the riches! That He would not only save us but include us! **35** That the Creator would hide Himself in the creature, so that the creature might find himself in the Creator! **36** To Him be the glory, not as a distant King, but as the Source, the Sustainer, and the Substance of our very lives, forever and ever. Amen.

  

**37** But some will say, "If we are already righteous, why then the Law? Why the commandments? Why the call to holiness?" **38** Hear this, for here lies the stumbling block of the religious.

Prayer is the communication between the Mother and the Child within. God's mercy is "womb-love"—the biological commitment to that which is part of His own substance. See Isaiah 49:15.
