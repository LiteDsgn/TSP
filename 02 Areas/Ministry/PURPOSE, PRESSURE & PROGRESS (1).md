## PART ONE: THE SEED IN THE SOIL (7 minutes)

### The Story We've Been Told vs. The Story That's Actually Happening

Open your Bibles to **John 12:24**. Jesus says something that will change how you see your current season:

> _"Truly, truly, I say to you, unless a grain of wheat falls into the earth and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it bears much fruit."_

Now, let me ask you: When a seed falls into the soil, what happens?

- It gets covered in darkness
    
- It experiences pressure from every side
    
- It looks like it's dying
    
- From the outside, it looks like it's been buried
    

But here's what we miss: **The seed is not being buried. The seed is being planted.**

### Your Life Right Now

Many of you are in university, or you just graduated. Some of you are in that space where:

- You've sent 50 CVs and nobody is replying
    
- Your mates are getting engaged and you're still waiting
    
- You have dreams but no clear path
    
- You have vision but it feels like you're stuck in the dark
    

And well-meaning people (including Christians) keep telling you, _"Just pray harder. Just have faith. Just wait on God."_

But nobody explains **why** the waiting feels so heavy.

**Here's the truth:** You're not stuck. You're germinating.

### The Purpose of Pressure

Look at **Romans 8:28-30**:

> _"And we know that for those who love God all things work together for good, for those who are called according to his purpose. For those whom he foreknew he also predestined to be conformed to the image of his Son."_

Notice the phrase: **"Conformed to the image of His Son."**

God is not trying to make you comfortable. God is trying to make you into **the image of Christ**.

And how did Christ become Christ? Through pressure:

- Born in a manger (pressure)
    
- Tempted in the wilderness (pressure)
    
- Misunderstood by His family (pressure)
    
- Betrayed by His friends (pressure)
    
- Crucified on a cross (ultimate pressure)
    

But after all that pressure? **Resurrection. Glory. Fruit.**

The pressure you're experiencing is not random. The pressure is **breaking the shell** so the life inside you can emerge.

**Illustration:** When you plant a seed, what keeps it in the soil? The weight of the earth pressing down. If you remove that pressure too early, the seed never germinates. The darkness and the pressure are **necessary** for the transformation.

## PART TWO: GOD'S QUESTION IN YOUR PRESSURE (8 minutes)

### Where Are You?

Turn with me to **Genesis 3:9**. After [[ADAM|Adam]] sinned and hid himself, God asked him a question:

> _"Where are you?"_

Now, this is interesting. God is omnipresentâ€”He knows everything. So why is He asking, "Where are you?"

Because the question is not geographical. **The question is ontological.** God is asking:

_"What state of being do you think you're in? Do you realize where you actually are? Do you understand what's happening to you?"_

### The Real Question God Is Asking You Today

When you're under pressureâ€”when the job isn't coming, when the relationship isn't working out, when your peers seem to be progressing and you feel left behindâ€”God is asking you the same question:

**"Where are you? What do you think is happening to you?"**

Your answer determines everything.

**Wrong Answer:** "I'm stuck. I'm failing. I'm behind. God has forgotten me."

**Right Answer:** "I'm planted. I'm being prepared. I'm in the soil, and God is working on me in the dark where nobody can see."

### The Nigerian Context: "When Will You...?"

Let's be honest. In Nigeria, the pressure is real:

- "When will you finish school?"
    
- "When will you get a job?"
    
- "When will you marry?"
    
- "When will you build a house?"
    
- "When will you have children?"
    

And when you're in your 20s or early 30s and you haven't checked these boxes yet, people look at you like something is wrong.

But here's what I want you to understand: **God's timing is not Nigerian timing.**

God doesn't operate on our cultural calendar. God operates on the principle of **maturity**, not speed.

Look at **Galatians 4:1-2**:

> _"I mean that the heir, as long as he is a child, is no different from a slave, though he is the owner of everything, but he is under guardians and managers until the date set by his father."_

You are an heir. You already own everything in Christ. But there's a **date set by the Father** for when you receive it in fullness.

Your job is not to rush the date. Your job is to **trust the Father** while you're being prepared.

## PART THREE: WHAT GOD IS BUILDING (8 minutes)

### You Are Not a Project. You Are a Temple.

**1 Corinthians 3:16** says:

> _"Do you not know that you are God's temple and that God's Spirit dwells in you?"_

God is not trying to fix you. God is trying to **build** you.

When you're under pressure in your career, in your relationships, in your personal growthâ€”God is not punishing you. God is **constructing something**.

### Three Things God Is Building In You

#### 1. **Structural Integrity ([[TAMIM|Tamim]])**

This is a Hebrew word that means "complete," "whole," "integrated."

When God says, "Be perfect" (Matthew 5:48), He doesn't mean "never make a mistake." He means "be wholeâ€”let your inside match your outside."

The pressure you're facing is revealing where you're fractured:

- Where you say one thing but do another
    
- Where you present one image on social media but live another reality privately
    
- Where you claim to trust God but you're anxious all the time
    

God is using this season to make you **whole**â€”so that the person you are in public is the same person you are in private.

**Practical example:** When you keep getting rejected from jobs, God may be asking, "Are you seeking your identity in your career, or in Me?" The pressure reveals the crack, so God can seal it.

#### 2. **Capacity to Carry Glory ([[KADOSH|Kadosh]])**

This Hebrew word means "holy," but it literally means "set apart" or "insulated."

Think about it like this: If you want to carry high voltage electricity, you need proper wiring. If the wiring is weak, the power will destroy the system.

God is preparing you to carry **glory**. But before He gives you the platform, the influence, the money, the marriage, the successâ€”He has to make sure you can handle it without it destroying you.

**Nigerian illustration:** How many people do we know who "blew" (got money, got fame) and then crashed? Why? Because they got the blessing before they had the capacity to carry it.

The pressure you're in right now is God **upgrading your wiring** so that when the blessing comes, it won't destroy you.

**James 1:2-4** says:

> _"Count it all joy, my brothers, when you meet trials of various kinds, for you know that the testing of your faith produces steadfastness. And let steadfastness have its full effect, that you may be perfect and complete, lacking in nothing."_

#### 3. **Rest in the Process ([[MENUHA|Menuha]])**

This is the Hebrew word for "rest."

But it doesn't mean "doing nothing." It means **"non-striving."**

It means trusting that God is the one working, not you.

**Matthew 11:28-30**:

> _"Come to me, all who labor and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest. Take my yoke upon you, and learn from me, for I am gentle and lowly in heart, and you will find rest for your souls. For my yoke is easy, and my burden is light."_

When you're strivingâ€”sending CVs obsessively, refreshing your phone for responses, comparing yourself to others on Instagramâ€”you're operating in **anxiety**, not rest.

But when you trust that God is the Architect, that He's building you, that the pressure is **purposeful**â€”you enter rest.

And **rest is attractive**. People are drawn to people who have peace in the storm.

### The Blueprint Is Christ

Here's the key: God is not building you randomly. God is building you into **the image of Christ**.

**Ephesians 1:4** says:

> _"He chose us in him before the foundation of the world, that we should be holy and blameless before him."_

Before you were born, before Nigeria existed, before time beganâ€”God chose you **in Christ**.

That means:

- Everything Christ has, you have
    
- Everything Christ is, you are becoming
    
- The pressure Christ went through, you're going throughâ€”but you're not alone
    

**Romans 8:17**:

> _"And if children, then heirsâ€”heirs of God and fellow heirs with Christ, provided we suffer with him in order that we may also be glorified with him."_

You're not suffering because you're cursed. You're suffering **with Christ** so you can be **glorified with Christ**.

## PART FOUR: PRACTICAL STEPS â€” TRUSTING GOD IN THE PRESSURE (4 minutes)

### 1. Change Your Perspective

Stop saying, "I'm stuck." Start saying, "I'm planted."

Stop saying, "God has forgotten me." Start saying, "God is working on me in the dark."

**Psalm 139:15-16**:

> _"My frame was not hidden from you, when I was being made in secret, intricately woven in the depths of the earth. Your eyes saw my unformed substance; in your book were written, every one of them, the days that were formed for me, when as yet there was none of them."_

God sees you even when you feel invisible. The "secret place" is where God does His best work.

### 2. Let the Pressure Teach You

Instead of resisting the pressure, ask:

- "God, what are You trying to build in me?"
    
- "What weakness is this revealing?"
    
- "What character are You forming?"
    

**2 Corinthians 12:9-10**:

> _"But he said to me, 'My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness.' Therefore I will boast all the more gladly of my weaknesses, so that the power of Christ may rest upon me. The pressure is making you **strong**.

### 3. Stop Comparing Your Chapter to Someone Else's Chapter

Everyone's seed germinates at a different rate.

Some seeds take 7 days. Some take 40 days. Some take years.

But **every seed that is planted in good soil will eventually grow**.

Your timeline is not your friend's timeline. And that's okay.

**Ecclesiastes 3:1**:

> _"For everything there is a season, and a time for every matter under heaven."_

### 4. Trust the Finished Work

Here's the mystery: **In God's eyes, you're already complete.**

**Colossians 2:10**:

> _"And you have been filled in him, who is the head of all rule and authority."_

You're not trying to **become** complete. You're waking up to the fact that **in Christ, you already are complete**.

The pressure is just revealing what was always true.
