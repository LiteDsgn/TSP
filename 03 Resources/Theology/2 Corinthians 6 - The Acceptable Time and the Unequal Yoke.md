# 2 Corinthians 6 - The Acceptable Time and the Unequal Yoke

If **Chapter 4** was about **THE TREASURE IN JARS OF CLAY** (suffering that reveals glory), and **Chapter 5** was about **THE MINISTRY OF RECONCILIATION** (ambassadors declaring the finished work), then **Chapter 6** is about **THE LIFE OF THE RECONCILER** (what it actually looks like to live as God's servant in a broken world).

This chapter divides into **TWO MAJOR SECTIONS**:

1. **Verses 1-13:** The credentials of authentic ministry (endurance, integrity, love)
2. **Verses 14-18:** The call to separation (the unequal yoke, being distinct)

Both sections are about **MAINTAINING THE INTEGRITY OF THE VESSEL** (Tamim) while **OPERATING IN A HOSTILE ENVIRONMENT**.

Let's go.

### **Unpacking the Final Paradox**

**"As dying, and behold, we live."**

This is **JOHN 12:24** enacted:

> "Unless a grain of wheat falls into the earth and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it bears much fruit."

**Paul is the SEED in the SOIL:**

- **DYING** (the shell is breaking ‚Äî outer man wasting away)
- **Yet LIVING** (the Life is emerging ‚Äî inner man being renewed)

**"As punished, yet not killed."**

**The Greek word PAIDEUOMENOI (œÄŒ±ŒπŒ¥ŒµœÖœåŒºŒµŒΩŒøŒπ)** comes from **PAIS (œÄŒ±·øñœÇ)** ‚Äî child.

**Hebrews 12:6** ‚Äî "For the Lord disciplines the one he loves, and chastises every son whom he receives."

**The pressure Paul experiences is not DESTRUCTIVE (killing).** **The pressure is FORMATIVE (disciplining).**

**"As sorrowful, yet always rejoicing."**

**This is the PARACONSISTENT OPERATION:**

**SORROW (external circumstances ‚Äî Keruvim pressure):**

- Paul grieves over churches struggling
- He feels pain from persecution
- He experiences the crushing of the shell

**JOY (internal reality ‚Äî Ruach sustaining):**

- Paul rejoices in the YESH he possesses
- He celebrates the FRUIT being produced
- He experiences MENUHA (rest) in God's finished work

**Both are true SIMULTANEOUSLY.**

**CHRIST'S PATTERN:**
